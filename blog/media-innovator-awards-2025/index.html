Incremento Wins Two Media Innovator Awards 2025

We don't do a lot of self-congratulation here. We're more interested in what we ship than what we say about ourselves. But this one felt worth sharing — not just because winning two awards is a good morning, but because of what they represent about the direction we've been building in.

The Media Innovator Awards 2025 have recognised Incremento with two distinctions: Best Digital Marketing Agency 2025 — Portugal and the Excellence Award for Web Design Services 2025. The awards celebrate creativity and innovation across media and communications, and are assessed through a merit-led research and evaluation process. We're genuinely pleased to be among the recipients this year.

What "Best Digital Marketing Agency in Portugal" actually means to us

Portugal's digital marketing scene has matured significantly over the last five years. Lisbon in particular has attracted a wave of international talent, remote-first companies and ambitious local brands all competing for the same audience attention. The bar has risen — and that's a good thing.

When we think about what it means to be the best digital marketing agency in Portugal, we're not thinking about award plaques. We're thinking about the briefs that come in from brands who've been burned before — by agencies that charged premium rates, delivered average results and moved on. We're thinking about the client in Príncipe Real who finally started ranking for the terms that matter after years of stagnation. The Cascais-based e-commerce brand whose cost-per-acquisition dropped 41% in twelve months. The Lisbon startup that finally understood its own analytics.

That's the work. That's what this recognition is really about.

"Great digital marketing isn't a campaign. It's a compounding system — and it takes patience, instrumentation and a team willing to own the outcome, not just the deliverable."

Our approach to digital marketing is rooted in three things: technical rigour, creative quality and a genuine obsession with measurement. We don't pitch strategies we can't track. We don't report on metrics that don't move revenue. And we don't disappear after launch.

The web design recognition — and why design alone is never enough

The Excellence Award for Web Design Services is one we're particularly proud of, because good web design is genuinely hard to do well — and easy to do badly while looking competent.

Lisbon has extraordinary visual design talent. Walk through any creative agency in Chiado or Santos and you'll find beautiful work. What's rarer is beautiful work that also loads in under two seconds, scores in the green on Core Web Vitals, converts meaningfully and holds up six months after launch when the client's team starts adding content.

Our design philosophy has always been conversion-led first, visually strong second — though in practice, the two aren't in conflict. A site that performs well is usually a site that's been thought through properly: clear hierarchy, fast load times, purposeful calls to action, accessible markup. The aesthetic follows from the function.

  • Performance by default — every site we build is optimised for Core Web Vitals from day one, not retrofitted
  • Design systems over one-off sites — we build components that scale as the brand grows
  • SEO baked in — structure, metadata, schema and crawlability are part of the design process, not an afterthought
  • Accessibility as standard — WCAG compliance isn't optional; it's good engineering
  • Outcomes tracked — we instrument every site we launch and review performance at 30, 60 and 90 days

A note on what's next

We started Incremento because we believed a senior, outcomes-focused studio could do things differently in Portugal. Ten years and 120+ projects later, the model holds. But the landscape keeps shifting — and the most significant shift underway right now is AI.

Through Incremento Labs, our applied AI practice, we've been spending the last two years embedding AI into marketing workflows, building custom agents for client operations and helping teams understand where AI creates real leverage versus where it's just noise. The results have been significant — and they're only getting more so.

The companies that will win the next decade of digital aren't the ones that adopt AI last, or the ones that adopt it sloppily. They're the ones that combine taste, rigour and the right technical foundation. That's what we're building towards — and what these awards reflect back at us.
